ELEPHANT
Elephants are the largest land animals in the world. A female elephant takes an impressive 22 months from conception to give birth, longer than any other animal. Newborn elephants can stand up shortly after birth and weigh up to 260 pounds. An elephant’s trunk is a remarkable tool with over 150,000 muscles and tendons.

TIGERS
Tigers once roamed over much of Central, South, and Southeast Asia, even reaching Turkey. Sadly, they are now found only in parts of South and Southeast Asia, and pockets in Eastern Russia and China. The Bengal tiger is the most well-known, and you might recognize Shere Khan from The Jungle Book.
